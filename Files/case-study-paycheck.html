Paycheck Stories

I designed and built Paycheck Stories as a personal product exploring how people remember and share their first earning moments.

Context

Sharing personal stories is harder than it looks.

People don't struggle to write. They struggle to start.

When it comes to first earnings, memories are emotional, not structured. Users hesitate before typing the first line. Traditional forms feel transactional.

Most platforms fail because they ask too many questions upfront, present empty screens, or treat storytelling like data entry.

People don't struggle to write. They struggle to start.

The Problem

How might we reduce hesitation and help users naturally recall and share their first earning story?

The Solution

A guided, low-pressure storytelling flow designed to help users ease into sharing instead of asking them to "write".

The Experience

A guided journey from memory to story — designed to reduce hesitation at every step.

01 — Entry The opening question sets a reflective tone before anything else loads.
02 — Emotion Users pick how the moment felt — no typing needed.
03 — Share your story The prompt that invites users to begin writing.
04 — Work identity Nine categories to feel seen — beyond just tech.
05 — Name Pick a suggested name or write your own — no real identity required.
06 — Write A notepad with floating lines — triggers memory without interrupting flow.
07 — Discover Stories grouped by emotion — proud, tough, quiet, unexpected.

Reading Experience

Designed for focus, not browsing. Most platforms show stories in grids. I intentionally avoided that.

  • Single story view — clicking a story opens it into a focused reading state, removing distractions and increasing emotional engagement
  • Natural exit interaction — clicking outside the story returns to the feed, avoiding heavy modal UI
  • Social proof without noise — each story shows a view count for validation, avoiding performative metrics like likes or comments
  • Emotional categorization — instead of generic filters, stories are grouped by feelings: Proud moment, Tough beginning, Quiet win, Unexpected start

Retention Design

A persistent layer titled "Your entry" tracks your first visit, your position (e.g. 235th visitor), and your contribution. This creates ownership, encourages return visits, and works without login friction.

Small UX decisions change behavior — removing the blank state increased writing comfort, identity selection improved expression, subtle floating lines improved recall.

Key Decisions

  • Less structure → more expression — avoided mandatory fields, amounts earned, and rigid inputs. Prioritized storytelling over data collection.
  • No social mechanics — no likes, no comments. Keeps the experience reflective, not performative.
  • Minimal UI — dark interface, soft highlights, centered layout. Keeps focus on the content.

Outcome

  • Reduced hesitation at writing entry
  • Encouraged deeper, more personal stories
  • Created a calm and focused storytelling space

What's next

  • Richer memory attachments (audio, receipts, etc.)
  • Deeper personalization in reading
  • Long-term story collections
