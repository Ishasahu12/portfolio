Product Design / Platform

Creative Platform

Redesigning the experience for artists and creators to share and discover work effortlessly.

Role Product Designer
Timeline 12 Weeks
Year 2024
Team 3 Designers

Overview

Artists and creators needed a platform to share their work, but existing options were either too complex, too cluttered, or focused more on social features than the work itself. The creative process deserved better.

We set out to build a portfolio-first platform where the work is the hero — clean galleries, minimal friction to share, and discovery that feels like browsing a gallery, not scrolling a feed.

The Problem

  • Portfolio displays were buried under feeds, likes, and follower counts
  • No clean way to organize work into meaningful collections
  • The "share" flow required too many steps before work was visible
  • Discovery was algorithmic rather than curated by human taste

Creative work speaks for itself. The best platform is one that gets out of the way.

The Solution

A portfolio-first platform where the work is the hero. Clean grid layouts, one-tap sharing, and curated discovery that surfaces gems from emerging creators.

The Experience

A portfolio-first platform where creators share work effortlessly and discovery feels like browsing a gallery.

01 — Portfolio grid Clean gallery layout where work speaks for itself — no likes, no follower counts.
02 — Collections Organize work into meaningful groups — portraits, street, abstract.
03 — Profile Clean creator identity — name, handle, and work in one view.
04 — Discover Curated discovery that surfaces gems — human taste, not algorithms.
05 — Upload Drag and drop — no steps, no friction.
06 — Publish One tap to go live — 30 seconds from files to portfolio.
07 — Analytics Simple metrics that matter — views and growth, nothing else.
